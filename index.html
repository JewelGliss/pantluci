Hoóba

Hoóba (/çoi̯˨˦.bä˨/) is a constructed language that aims to explore the bounds of what a human-usable language can be by using a breadth-first inspired grammar which appears in few if any natural or constructed languages. To make up for the unfamiliarity, it uses a largely simple grammar that attempts to minimize the number of things a speaker or listener needs to think about.

Hoóba grammar is syntactically very simple, relying on only a handful of rules.

  • "a" always requires exactly two arguments and other words never accept any arguments.
  • "-n" is a clitic form of "a". (written as a prefix of the following word with "n-" when preceeding a word starting in /ʔ/)
  • "á" is an alternate form of "a".
  • Words are fill the oldest unfilled argument slot.
  • Only certain combinations of word types may legally fill the slots of an "a".
