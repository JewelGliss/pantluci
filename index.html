Hýyban is a constructed language that aims to explore the bounds of what a human-usable language can be. Hýyban uses a breadth-first grammar which appears in few if any natural or constructed languages. To make up for the alienity, it uses a largely simple grammar that attempts to minimize the amount of things a speaker or listener needs to think about.

Hýyban grammar is syntactically very simple, relying on only a handful of rules.

  • Particles always accept exactly two arguments; and non-particles, also called leafs, never accept any arguments.
  • Words are assigned as a tail to the oldest unfilled argument slot.
  • A sentence with remaining unfilled slots is grammatically incorrect until when all the slots are filled and the sentence is finished.
