Hoóban

Hoóban (/çoi̯˨˦.bä˨n/) is an experimental constructed language designed to explore the bounds of what a human-usable language can be by using a breadth-first inspired grammar which appears in few if any natural or constructed languages. To make up for the unfamiliarity, it uses a largely simple grammar that attempts to minimize the number of things a speaker or listener needs to think about. The language was developed beginning in late June 2021 following various sketches of potential breadth-first languages inspired by xkcd 2407. It originally used a modified consonant inventory from unrelated language sketches from December 2020, a vowel inventory copied from Lojban, a tone system copied from Láadan, and a grammar system heavily inspired by toki pona. By early 2022 most of these influences were largely obscured by revisions to the language.

Hoóban is intended to explore many linguistic theories that claim some form of fundamental or underlying genetic language or grammar. This language violates most if not all of their proposed systems, so if it can be learned to fluency then those theories are likely false. However, Hoóban is a hobby project at best and should not be taken seriously enough to be considered actual science. It exists to be interesting and entertaining, so no enforced standard of Hoóban will ever have official support, and speakers will be encouraged to speak how they wish to.

Morphology

Syllables in Hoóban are of the form CV(N), i.e. consonant + vowel (accompanied by tone) + optional final nasal stop.

Word weight in Hoóban is crucial to understanding word boundaries and can be calculated by adding up the total number of non-mid vowels (ä, i, u), voiced consonants, and dual tones. Syllables bind to the prior syllables to form multisyllabic words if the prior syllables collectively weigh less than two. When the collective weight of the syllables becomes two or more, the word ends and a new word can begin.

Allophony and Phonotactics

The nasal at the end of a syllable is assimilated to the following consonant's place of articulation and must be dissimilated away from the following consonant's place of articulation if the following consonant is a nasal.

The same vowel may not occur on both sides of a glottal stop word-internally.

The same vowel may not occur on both sides of a nasal stop followed by a glottal stop word-internally.

/n/ may not occur as an onset.

Orthography

Hoóban is written using the Latin script. With capitalization only on the first letter of every sentence. The word "en" may be written as a dot (‧), otherwise punctuation does not appear. The acute accent may be written as an apostrophe following the letter it's attached to, or if that is not possible as "y". "ı" and "ȷ" may be written as "i" and "j" respectively if needed. The acute accent is considered its own letter.

Grammar

Syntax

Hoóban grammar is syntactically very simple, relying on only a handful of rules.

  • "eén" is a contraction of "eé e".
  • "eé" is a contraction of "en en".
  • "en" always requires exactly two arguments and other words never accept any arguments.
  • Words are fill the oldest unfilled argument slot.
  • Only certain combinations of word types may legally fill the slots of an "en".

Syntactic category combinations

Lexical items may be adverbs, conjunctions, nouns, or verbs.

Below are possible combinations of types the arguments of "en" may be, followed by "↦", the type it acts as, ":", and a description of the semantics.

noun, verb ↦ noun: noun which is the subject of verb.

verb noun ↦ verb: verb which has noun as object.

(if unspecified the object can be assumed to be the cause/agent)

conjunction, any type ↦ CP: used in the following rule.

any type, CP (must contain the same type) ↦ the same type: conjoins two of the same types.

verb, adverb ↦ verb: verbs happens in a manner described by the adverb.

adverb, adverb ↦ adverb: 1st adverb is true in a manner described by the 2nd adverb.

Vocabulary

Hoóban words are taken from a wide range of languages with the only concerns being whether the word is needed and how much it needs to be modified to obey proper morphology. This means that commonly spoken languages like English, Mandarin Chinese, or Spanish are not given special consideration and are often passed over entirely. The semantic domains of the borrowed word are also mostly carried over with a small level of modification to remove any wholly disconnected polysemy and to simply appease the desires of whoever is loaning the word.

Lexical phrases

Lexical items in Hoóban are not necessarily a single root, and may instead consist of any syntactically valid (ignoring types) construction. These multi word lexical items may also have empty spots in their defintions that must be filled when used.

Typical names in Hoóban are lexical phrases with a noun as the first node.

Names and loans

Loan words, including proper nouns, are typically converted to Hoóban's morphology one of two ways:

  • attempting to maximize the recognizability of the pronunciation (the more common method)
  • attempting to maximize the recognizability of the spelling (used only when the spelling of the original word is extremely close to being a valid as a Hýyban word and the pronunciation of the word is not nearly close to being a valid Hýyban word)

Additionally, when using the first method, a few specific recommendations may be followed:

  • vowel height distinctions may be swapped to manipulate the weight of the syllables
  • voice distinctions may be swapped to manipulate the weight of the syllables
  • swapping vowels is preferred to swapping consonants unless the vowel of the word being loaned is a perfect match for a Hýyban vowel.
  • diphthongs may become dual tones inheriting their first vowel and rising or lowering depending on the vowel weight change of the diphthong (au => áa)
  • if diphthongs are comprised of vowels sharing a weight they may become dual tones inheriting their first vowel and rising or lowering depending on the vowel height change of the diphthong (ai => aá)
  • it is preferable to drop a syllable than to gain one unless dropping a syllable would result in a single syllable word
  • low tone may be treated as default with dual tones being used to transcribe vowel length, or high tone to transcribe stress
  • phonemes less common in Hýyban are preferred to more common ones if the word being borrowed has multiple valid pronunciations in the original language
  • unvoiced consonant clusters may become ejectives, and voiced consonant clusters may become implosives

Introduction examples

Generally a good way to learn a language is to understand the basics, use that basic understanding to learn some vocabulary, and as soon as possible start learning via immersion. This language is intentionally designed so that it is difficult to explain using tradtional structures, so this document attempts to mirror that learning structure in an attempt at documenting the language. So far this document has covered the the basics, the following section indroduces a handfull of vocabulary, then a section uses that introductory vocab to teach new words through immersion.

Remeber that en requires exactly two arguments and behaves differently depending on the types of those arguments. In the examples above, those types are a noun followed by a verb, and which means the noun experiencing the verb.

En ı́n en laá mı - I like you

Here you can see how en can be an argument for another en. in these examples the first en functions the same as in previous sentences. While the second one has a verb followed by a noun, and as a result acts as a verb done by the noun.

En mı en laá en kı́tı́ mú - This cat likes me

En ı́n en laá en kı́tı́ lowen - A big cat like you

These are nearly identical to the previous block, but this time the second noun is also experiencing a verb. This is very similar to how adjectives work in english.

Eé hónre kı́tı́ ı́n - Your cat is red

Eé mı kı́tı́ lowen - A big cat is mine

Remember that eé is a contraction of en en. Also keep in mind that words fill the oldest available argument, so the word order may be different from what you are used to.

Eén kı́tı́ mı laá ı́n - You like my cat

Eén kı́tı́ lowen laá mı - I like a big cat

This is the same as above but with a transitive verb.
