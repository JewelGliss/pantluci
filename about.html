However, long before the direct influence of these languages, a couple of languages got me hooked on the potential of what a language could be. The very first one I encountered is admittedly not a conlang in the traditional usage of the word is an esoteric programming language, Piet, that not only avoids using text but also operates using stack manipulation. This introduction of messing with assumed standards of language by using abnormal data structures is probably what lead to my interest in a breadth-first language in the first place.

From there I found my way to Frathwiki and discovered Fith and the concepts of arithmographic languages, breifscripts, and speedtalks. Breifscripts and speedtalks are languages designed to either be written or spoken faster than normal. Through the years I have thoroughly explored these concepts always determining that the bottleneck on communication speed is having something to say or waiting for the listener to comprehend what has been said rather than language. So if this is a solvable problem, someone else will have to be the one to solve it. An arithmographic language is one where words can be unambiguously converted into integers where the prime factors of that integer suggest the meaning of the word. Despite numerous attempts I have yet to come to any conclusions on the practicality of this concept, it is likely my next topic to explore after Tsohci. Finally, Fith is a conlang based around a stack-based data structure. In many ways, despite stack-based and breadth-first being seemingly incongruent terms, Fith has the same initial feeling of inescapably alien, seemingly random word order that is the main focus of Pantluci.

Eventually, I found my way into the worlds of Lojban, toki pona, and r/Conlangs. From there I discovered languages with almost twice as many tones as vowels and consonants combined, languages that were designed to compress language into bytes in order to more effectively store it, mixed spoken signed languages, languages written with koi fish, languages with "stances" that change how you talk depending on how authoritative you are on the topic, and so much more. Throughout all of this, I played with different ideas for my own languages: lambda calculus turned into a human language, languages with the fewest possible phonemes without violating proposed universals, languages with multiple parallel streams of information, etc. Few of them lasted more than a week. The ones that did never lasted more than a month. Pantluci is the first language I have been truly happy with. It has been the source of many long nights, rabbit holes, and debates, and has probably annoyed eveyone I am close to with my ramblings a great deal. So, my final shoutout is to you, whoever you are reading this for taking the time to learn about what brought me so much joy.

definition of possible language:
any set of rules pairing concepts to strings which could theoretically exist

definition of functional language:
any possible lang that can make any finitely detailed contrast (rather than express any concept)
more concretely for any pair of non-identical concepts, some finite string can include one but exclude the other

definition of practical language:
functional language which is perfect system (exactly as complicated as it needs to be)

definition of language family:
not the same as normal, refers to every point on the contium of dialects as related rather than whole groups

Pantluci Overview

Pantluci ([păn.t͡ʃɹu.ci]) is an experimental constructed language designed to explore the bounds of what a human-usable language can be by using a breadth-first inspired grammar which appears in few if any natural or constructed languages. To make up for the unfamiliarity, it uses a largely simple grammar that attempts to minimize the number of things a speaker or listener needs to think about. The language was developed beginning in late June 2021 following various sketches of potential breadth-first languages inspired by xkcd 2407. It originally used a modified consonant inventory from unrelated language sketches from December 2020, a vowel inventory copied from Lojban, a tone system copied from Láadan, and a grammar system heavily inspired by toki pona. By early 2022 most of these influences were largely obscured by revisions to the language in an attempt to minimize the chances of sound changes, and the wide variety of gramatical particles were reduced as much as possible. By mid June 2022 the now sole particle used in the language being extremely repetitive became a larger and larger problem forcing a redo of the phonology in order to allow for less repetive forms and a greater focus on aesthetics. By July 2022, the language expanded into a research project exploring esoteric languages in general, with the language used as an example. Its phonology was once again scrapped and rebuilt with a focus on filling the same purpose as the grammar. By January 2023 its minmal inventory had made reduplication equaly as repetitive, and it was changed to a virst vowel umlaut system that mirrored the recently implemented compounding system. Over the course of its devolpment it went through the names Hýyban ([çə˦.ə˨.bä˨n]), Hoóban ([çoi̯˨˦.bä˨n]), Hoóba ([çoi̯˨˦.bä˨]), Tsohci ([t͜soː.cʰi]), and now Pantluci

minimal phonology
The smallest claimed consonant inventory of any natrual language doculect is the Proto-Lakes Plain Language with a meer five (Clouse).
If reconstructions are ignored, the smallest one is a tie between Iau, Kirikiri, and Rotokas with six (SOURCES).
Either way this should be suffienct to show that while odd, Pantluci's consonant inventory is not unreasonable.
Three vowel systems are common enough that they should need no justification.
Although the combination of a small consonant and small vowel inventory may seems strange, Pirahã shares this trait at eight consonants and three vowels (SOURCE).
This reasonable but not impossible phonology is likely something that would never evolve natrually, as any mergers that would shrink an inventory to this point likely would destroy the lexicon to an irreprable state.

Pantluci is intended to explore many linguistic theories that claim some form of fundamental or underlying genetic language or grammar and act as a language outside of the typical lingustic universals. This language violates most if not all of their proposed systems, so if it can be learned to fluency then those theories are likely innacurate. While Pantluci is a project with countless hours of work behind it no experiment is complete without contiued indpendent reproduction and therefore it should not be taken seriously enough to be considered in any way groundbreaking. It exists to be test the practicality of its weirdness, so no enforced standard of Pantluci will ever have official support, and speakers will be encouraged to speak how they feel most comfortable.

This description of the language will not fully explain or document the entiriety of the language, however it should prove as a suffienct baseline to prove the viability of the language and potentially spark intrest.

Pantlucian Morphology

Syllables in Pantluci are of the form (t)(C)V(n), i.e. an optional /t/ followed by an optional consonant followed by a vowel or syllabic consonant followed by a nasal coda. Words may be any combination of syllables with the following restrictions:

  • /p/, /t/, or /k/ may act as though they were syllables during word construction
  • /pp/, and /kk/ are illegal sequences
  • /tt/ may not occur before a non-syllabic consonant
  • syllables with syllabic consonants may not have a coda
  • words may not end with /p/, /t/, or /k/
  • /B/ should first be interpreted as an onset, then a coda of the preceeding syllable, then a nucleus
  • /D/ should first be interpreted as an intial onset, then a nucleus
  • the second syllable may not start with a vowel
  • any syllable following a coda may not start with a vowel
  • stress rests on the first syllable containing a vowel
  • words must contain at least one vowel
  • the same vowel may not occur twice in a row
  • the same realization of a phoneme may not occur twice in a row

Words in Pantluci undergo a morphological process in order to denote the gramatical structure of the sentence. This process consists of replacing the first vowel:

  1. with /a/ if there are 0 preceeding nodes.
  2. with /i/ if there is 1 preceeding node.
  3. with /u/ if there are 2 preceeding nodes.
  4. with /ai/ if there are 3 preceeding nodes.
  5. with /au/ if there are 4 preceeding nodes.
  6. with /aia/ if there are 5 preceeding nodes.
  7. with /aua/ if there are 6 preceeding nodes.
  8. etc

Pantlucian Sentences

Pantlucian grammar is syntactically very simple, relying on only a handful of rules.

  • A "node" is an imaginary word that is inserted before words for each time they are reduplicated.
  • Nodes always requires exactly two arguments and other words never accept any arguments.
  • Words always fill the oldest unfilled argument slot.
  • Only certain combinations of word types may legally fill the slots of a node.

Lexical items may be adverbs, conjunctions, nouns, verbs, or postpositions.

Below are possible combinations of types the arguments of "node" may be, followed by "↦", the type it acts as, ":", and a description of the semantics.

noun, verb ↦ noun: noun with the verb attached as a relative clause. The noun is a typically but not always an experiencer, theme, or patient. This noun is refered to as the subject.

verb noun ↦ verb: verb with the noun incorporated. The noun is a typically but not always an agent, stimulus, or force. This noun is refered to as the object.

(in defintions where the object is unspecified, it can be assumed to be one of these)

conjunction, any type ↦ CP: used in the following rule.

any type, CP (must contain the same type) ↦ the same type: conjoins two of the same types.

verb, adverb ↦ verb: verbs happens in a manner described by the adverb.

adverb, adverb ↦ adverb: 1st adverb is true in a manner described by the 2nd adverb.

postposition, ?? ↦ postposition: 1st adverb is true in a manner described by the 2nd adverb.

verb, postposition ↦ verb: verbs happens in a manner described by the postposition. postposition behaves like adverb.

noun, postposition ↦ noun: noun with the postposition attached as a relative clause. postposition behaves like verb.

verb, pronoun ↦ verb: verb with the pronoun incorporated. pronoun behaves like noun.

noun, pronoun ↦ noun: noun with the verb attached as a relative clause meaning "subject is owned by [pronoun]". pronoun behaves like verb.

At the top level of a sentence, as well as within some special contexts, the semantics of a clause change somewhat.

Nouns are asserted to either exist, be contextually relavent, or awnser a question.

Verbs are asserted to either happen, be contextually relavent, or awnser a question.

Other clause types are asserted to be a good addition to a previous sentence, or awnser a question.

Notes

pantluci partially orginated from a random example word used to show how the language could use reduplication, however small modifications were made due to changes in phonology and in order to maintain a tradition of the language being named using its most unique features compared to the previous version, finally it was updated when the phonology was shrunken in order to have each phoneme exactly once.
