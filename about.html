Hoóba

Hoóba (/çoi̯˨˦.bä˨/) is an experimental constructed language designed to explore the bounds of what a human-usable language can be by using a breadth-first inspired grammar which appears in few if any natural or constructed languages. To make up for the unfamiliarity, it uses a largely simple grammar that attempts to minimize the number of things a speaker or listener needs to think about. The language was developed beginning in late June 2021 following various sketches of potential breadth-first languages inspired by xkcd 2407. It originally used a modified consonant inventory from unrelated language sketches from December 2020, a vowel inventory copied from Lojban, a tone system copied from Láadan, and a grammar system heavily inspired by toki pona. By early 2022 most of these influences were largely obscured by revisions to the language.

Hoóba is intended to explore many linguistic theories that claim some form of fundamental or underlying genetic language or grammar. This language violates most if not all of their proposed systems, so if it can be learned to fluency then those theories are likely false. However, Hoóba is a hobby project at best and should not be taken seriously enough to be considered actual science. It exists to be interesting and entertaining, so no enforced standard of Hoóba will ever have official support, and speakers will be encouraged to speak how they wish to.

Morphology

Syllables in Hoóba are of the form CV(N), i.e. consonant + vowel (accompanied by tone) + optional final nasal stop.

Word weight in Hoóba is crucial to understanding word boundaries and can be calculated by adding up the total number of non-mid vowels (ä, i, u), voiced consonants, and dual tones. Syllables bind to the prior syllables to form multisyllabic words if the prior syllables collectively weigh less than two. When the collective weight of the syllables becomes two or more, the word ends and a new word can begin.

Allophony and Phonotactics

The nasal at the end of a syllable is assimilated to the following consonant's place of articulation and must be dissimilated away from the following consonant's place of articulation if the following consonant is a nasal.

The same vowel may not occur on both sides of a glottal stop word-internally.

The same vowel may not occur on both sides of a nasal stop followed by a glottal stop word-internally.

/n/ may not occur before /ʔ/ in the same word, word initially, or finally (with the exception of "-n").

/n.ʔ/ is realized as [.n].

Orthography

Hoóba is written using the Latin script with capitalization only on the first letter of every sentence. The acute accent may be written as an apostrophe following the letter it's attached to, or if that is not possible as "y". "ı" and "ȷ" may be written as "i" and "j" respectively if needed. The acute accent is considered its own letter.

Grammar

Syntax

Hoóba grammar is syntactically very simple, relying on only a handful of rules.

  • "a" always requires exactly two arguments and other words never accept any arguments.
  • "-n" is a clitic form of "a". (written as a prefix of the following word with "n-" when preceeding a word starting in /ʔ/)
  • "á" is an alternate form of "a".
  • Words are fill the oldest unfilled argument slot.
  • Only certain combinations of word types may legally fill the slots of an "a".

Syntactic category combinations

Lexical items may be adverbs, conjunctions, nouns, or verbs.

Below are possible combinations of types the arguments of "en" may be, followed by "↦", the type it acts as, ":", and a description of the semantics.

noun, verb ↦ noun: noun with the verb attached as a relative clause. The noun is a typically but not always an experiencer, theme, or patient. This noun is refered to as the subject.

verb noun ↦ verb: verb with the noun incorporated. The noun is a typically but not always an agent, stimulus, or force. This noun is refered to as the object.

(in defintions where the object is unspecified, it can be assumed to be one of these)

conjunction, any type ↦ CP: used in the following rule.

any type, CP (must contain the same type) ↦ the same type: conjoins two of the same types.

verb, adverb ↦ verb: verbs happens in a manner described by the adverb.

adverb, adverb ↦ adverb: 1st adverb is true in a manner described by the 2nd adverb.

Semantics

At the top level of a sentence, as well as within some lexical phrases, the semantics of a clause change somewhat.

Nouns are asserted to either exist, be contextually relavent, or awnser a question.

Verbs are asserted to either happen, be contextually relavent, or awnser a question.

Other clause types are asserted to be a good addition to a previous sentence, or awnser a question.

Vocabulary

Hoóba words are taken from a wide range of languages with the only concerns being whether the word is needed and how much it needs to be modified to obey proper morphology. This means that commonly spoken languages like English, Mandarin Chinese, or Spanish are not given special consideration and are often passed over entirely. The semantic domains of the borrowed word are also mostly carried over with a small level of modification to remove any wholly disconnected polysemy and to simply appease the desires of whoever is loaning the word.

Lexical phrases

Lexical items in Hoóba are not necessarily a single root, and may instead consist of any syntactically valid (ignoring types) construction. These multi word lexical items may also have an equal number of empty spots in their construction and defintions that must be filled when used.

Names and loans

Loan words, including proper nouns, are typically converted to Hoóba's morphology one of two ways:

  • attempting to maximize the recognizability of the pronunciation (the more common method)
  • attempting to maximize the recognizability of the spelling (used only when the spelling of the original word is extremely close to being a valid as a Hoóba word and the pronunciation of the word is not nearly close to being a valid Hoóba word)

Additionally, when using the first method, a few specific recommendations may be followed:

  • vowel height distinctions may be swapped to manipulate the weight of the syllables
  • voice distinctions may be swapped to manipulate the weight of the syllables
  • swapping vowels is preferred to swapping consonants unless the vowel of the word being loaned is a perfect match for a Hoóba vowel.
  • diphthongs typically become dual tones inheriting their first vowel and rising or lowering depending on the vowel weight or hight change.
  • it is preferable to drop a syllable than to gain one unless dropping a syllable would result in a single syllable word
  • low tone may be treated as default with dual tones being used to transcribe vowel length, or high tone to transcribe stress
  • phonemes less common in Hoóba are preferred to more common ones if the word being borrowed has multiple valid pronunciations in the original language
  • unvoiced consonant clusters may become ejectives, and voiced consonant clusters may become implosives

Generally a good way to learn a language is to understand the basics, use that basic understanding to learn some vocabulary, and as soon as possible start learning via immersion. This language is intentionally designed so that it is difficult to explain using tradtional structures, so this document attempts to mirror that learning structure in an attempt at documenting the language. Here you can see how a, or in this case -n can be an argument for another a. in these examples the first a functions the same as in previous sentences. While the second one has a verb followed by a noun, and as a result acts as a verb with the noun incorporated. In other words the first sentence might be more accurately translated as "A me that is cat-liked exists", but this is even less like how people speak in English.

A vun laán kı́tı́ lowe - A big cat like you

These are nearly identical to the previous block, but this time the second noun is also experiencing a verb. This is very similar to how adjectives work in english.

numbers less than 100 can be regularly derived by finding the largest irregular digit lower than the desired digit with the same final base 10 digit and prefixing it with the appropriate prefix. powers of 100 can be constructed by prefixing a number witht́e or t́é (to match the following tone) to take a number to the power of 100. All other numbers are made using normal grammar treating numbers 0-99 as verbs, powers of 100 as nouns, and using váa to join digits typically from greatest to least. The noun that the final 0-99 digit is attached to is the thing being counted. negatives are formed by attaching débóo to that unit noun.
