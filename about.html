However, long before the direct influence of these languages, a couple of languages got me hooked on the potential of what a language could be. The very first one I encountered is admidtly not a conlang in the tradtional useage of the word is an esoteric programming language that not only avoids using text, but also operates using stack manipulation. This introduction of messing with assumed standards of language by using abnormal data structures is probably what lead to my interest in a breadth first language in the first place.

From there I found my way to Frathwiki and discovered Fith and the concepts of an arithmographic languages, breifscripts, and speedtalks. Breifscripts and speedtalks are languages designed to either be written or spoken faster than normal. Through the years I have thoroughly explored these concepts always deteriming that the bottleneck on communication speed is having something to say rather than language. So if this is a solveable problem, someone else will have to be the one to solve it. An arithmographic language is one where words can be unambigously converted into integers where the prime factors of that integer suggest at the meaning of the word. Dispite numerous attempts I have yet to come to any conclusions on the practicality of this concept, it is likely my next topic to explore after Tsohci. Finally, Fith is a conlang based around a stack based data structure. In many ways, dispite stack based and breadth first being seemingly incongruent terms, Fith has the same initial feeling of inescapably alien, seemingly random word order that is the main forcus of Pantluci.

Eventually I found my way into the worlds of Lojban, toki pona, and r/Conlangs. From there I discovered languages with 18 tones, 4 vowels, and 6 consonants, languages that were designed to compress language into bytes in order to more effectively store it, mixed spoken signed languages, languages written with koi fish, and so much more. Throughout all of this, I played with diffrent ideas for my own languages: lambda calc turned into a human language, languages with the fewest possible phonemes without violating proposed universals, languages with multiple parallel streams of information, etc. few of them lasted more than a week. The ones that did never lasted more than a month. Pantluci is the first language I have actually been happy with. (THINGS) So, my final shoutout is to you, whoever you are reading this for taking the time to learn about what brought me so much joy.

Pantluci Overview

Pantluci ([pan.t͡ʃɹu.ci]) is an experimental constructed language designed to explore the bounds of what a human-usable language can be by using a breadth-first inspired grammar which appears in few if any natural or constructed languages. To make up for the unfamiliarity, it uses a largely simple grammar that attempts to minimize the number of things a speaker or listener needs to think about. The language was developed beginning in late June 2021 following various sketches of potential breadth-first languages inspired by xkcd 2407. It originally used a modified consonant inventory from unrelated language sketches from December 2020, a vowel inventory copied from Lojban, a tone system copied from Láadan, and a grammar system heavily inspired by toki pona. By early 2022 most of these influences were largely obscured by revisions to the language in an attempt to minimize the chances of sound changes, and the wide variety of gramatical particles were reduced as much as possible. By mid June 2022 the now sole particle used in the language being extremely repretive became a larger and larger problem forcing a redo of the phonology in order to allow for less repetive forms and a greater focus on aesthetics. By July 2022, the language expanded into a research project exploring esoteric languages in general, with the language relegated to an example. Its phonology was once again scrapped and rebuilt with a focus on filling the same purpose as the grammar.

It went through the names Hýyban ([çə˦.ə˨.bä˨n]), Hoóban ([çoi̯˨˦.bä˨n]), Hoóba ([çoi̯˨˦.bä˨]), Tsohci ([t͜soː.cʰi]), and now Pantluci

Pantluci is intended to explore many linguistic theories that claim some form of fundamental or underlying genetic language or grammar and act as a language outside of the typical lingustic universals. This language violates most if not all of their proposed systems, so if it can be learned to fluency then those theories are likely innacurate. While Pantluci is a project with countless hours of work behind it no experiment is complete without contiued indpendent reproduction and therefore it should not be taken seriously enough to be considered in any way groundbreaking. It exists to be test the practicality of its weirdness, so no enforced standard of Pantluci will ever have official support, and speakers will be encouraged to speak how they feel most comfortable. 

minimal phonology
The smallest claimed consonant inventory of any natrual language doculect is the Proto-Lakes Plain Language with a meer five (Clouse).
If reconstructions are ignored, the smallest one is a tie between Iau, Kirikiri, and Rotokas with six (SOURCES).
Either way this should be suffienct to show that while odd, Pantluci's consonant inventory is not unreasonable.
Three vowel systems are common enough that they need no justification.
Although the combination of a small consonant and small vowel inventory may seems strange, Pirahã shares this trait at eight consonants and three vowels (SOURCE).
This reasonable but not impossible phonology is likely something that would never evolve natrually, as any mergers that would shrink an inventory to this point likely would destroy the lexicon to an irreprable state.

Pantlucian Morphology

Syllables in Pantluci are of the form (C)(d~ɹ~l)V(b~m), i.e. an optional consonant optionally followed by /d/ followed by a vowel, optionally followed by /b/. Words may be any combination of syllables with the following restrictions:

  • the first syllable may not be the same as the second syllable
  • the second syllable may not both begin with a vowel and have the same vowel as the previous syllable
  • the first syllable of a word may not be just a vowel
  • if /b/ could legally be interpreted as either a coda or onset, it is an onset
  • stress rests on the first syllable

Words in Pantluci undergo a single morphological process in order to denote the gramatical structure of the sentence. This process consists of two alternating stages:

  1. reduplicate the vowel of the first syllable immediately after it, then if it has a /b/ coda, move it to the new syllable.
  2. undo the previous step and fully reduplicate the first syllable immediately after it
pantluci → paantluci → panpantluci → paanpantluci → ...

Pantlucian Words

Pantlucian words are taken from a wide range of languages with the only concerns being whether the word is needed and how much it needs to be modified to obey proper morphology. This means that commonly spoken languages like English, Mandarin Chinese, or Spanish are not given special consideration and are often passed over entirely. The semantic domains of the borrowed word are also mostly carried over with a small level of modification to remove any wholly disconnected polysemy and to simply appease the desires of whoever is loaning the word.

Pantlucian Lexicalization

Lexical items in Pantluci are not necessarily a single root, and may instead consist of any syntactically valid (ignoring types) construction. These multi word lexical items may also have an equal number of empty spots in their construction and defintions that must be filled when used.

Pantlucian Sentences

Pantlucian grammar is syntactically very simple, relying on only a handful of rules.

  • A "node" is an imaginary word that is inserted before words for each time they are reduplicated.
  • Nodes always requires exactly two arguments and other words never accept any arguments.
  • Words always fill the oldest unfilled argument slot.
  • Only certain combinations of word types may legally fill the slots of a node.

Lexical items may be adverbs, conjunctions, nouns, or verbs.

Below are possible combinations of types the arguments of "en" may be, followed by "↦", the type it acts as, ":", and a description of the semantics.

noun, verb ↦ noun: noun with the verb attached as a relative clause. The noun is a typically but not always an experiencer, theme, or patient. This noun is refered to as the subject.

verb noun ↦ verb: verb with the noun incorporated. The noun is a typically but not always an agent, stimulus, or force. This noun is refered to as the object.

(in defintions where the object is unspecified, it can be assumed to be one of these)

conjunction, any type ↦ CP: used in the following rule.

any type, CP (must contain the same type) ↦ the same type: conjoins two of the same types.

verb, adverb ↦ verb: verbs happens in a manner described by the adverb.

adverb, adverb ↦ adverb: 1st adverb is true in a manner described by the 2nd adverb.

At the top level of a sentence, as well as within some lexical phrases, the semantics of a clause change somewhat.

Nouns are asserted to either exist, be contextually relavent, or awnser a question.

Verbs are asserted to either happen, be contextually relavent, or awnser a question.

Other clause types are asserted to be a good addition to a previous sentence, or awnser a question.

Notes

catuna from Kasona, a name that orginates from a clipping of kala sona from toki pona.

cunu orginated in a randomly generated placeholder vocabulary for the first version of Pantluci, at the time it was transcribed qúu as the languages morphology and orthography was updated, this word was kept to use as an example of the letter "q" even past the language having the letter.

nau partially orginated from a non-random vocabulary for a previous version of Pantluci, at the time it was transcribed bóo, and was used to demonstrate that version's naming system.

pantluci partially orginated from a random example word used to show how the language could use reduplication, however small modifications were made due to changes in phonology and in order to maintain a tradition of the language being named using its most unique features compared to the previous version, finally it was updated when the phonology was shrunken in order to have each letter exactly once.

piati from Piet, the name of an esoteric programming language designed to mimic the appearance of the works of Piet Mondrian. It was the first esoteric languages studied by Pantluci's creater who mispronounced it as [pʰi:.ʔɛtʰ] for far too long.

tatu from *ta-tou, a word from proto-polynesian which doesnt have an offical language code.

Introduction examples

Generally a good way to learn a language is to understand the basics, use that basic understanding to learn some vocabulary, and as soon as possible start learning via immersion. This language is intentionally designed so that it is difficult to explain using tradtional structures, so this document attempts to mirror that learning structure in an attempt at documenting the language. So far this document has covered the the basics, the following section indroduces about two hundred peices of vocabulary, with a break to introduce the number system in the middle, then a section uses that introductory vocab to teach new words through immersion.

(Number, definiteness, and other factors are not specified by default in Tsohci, even though the english translation may be forced to mark them.)

Remeber that sentence breaks requires exactly two arguments and behaves differently depending on the types of those arguments. In the examples above, those types are a noun followed by a verb, and which means the noun with the verb attached as a relative clause. Since the top level of these sentences are noun, the sentences assert that the described noun exist. In other words the first sentence might be more accurately translated as "A big cat exists" but since people dont talk like that in English, a slight less accurate, but pretty much semantically identical translation is listed instead.

Here you can see how a sentence break can be an argument for another sentence break. in these examples the first sentence break functions the same as in previous sentences. While the second one has a verb followed by a noun, and as a result acts as a verb with the noun incorporated. In other words the first sentence might be more accurately translated as "A me that is cat-liked exists", but this is even less like how people speak in English.

These are nearly identical to the previous block, but this time the second noun is also experiencing a verb. This is very similar to how adjectives work in english.

Keep in mind that words fill the oldest available argument, so the word order may be different from what you might expect.

Here you can see the reduplicated form of the sentence break following a word, otherwise this is the same as above but with a transitive verb.

numbers less than 100 can be regularly derived by finding the largest irregular digit lower than the desired digit with the same final base 10 digit and prefixing it with the appropriate prefix. powers of 100 can be constructed by prefixing a number witht́e or t́é (to match the following tone) to take a number to the power of 100. All other numbers are made using normal grammar treating numbers 0-99 as verbs, powers of 100 as nouns, and using váa to join digits typically from greatest to least. The noun that the final 0-99 digit is attached to is the thing being counted. negatives are formed by attaching débóo to that unit noun.
