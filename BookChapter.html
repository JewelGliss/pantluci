8.1 Overview
Pantluci (English [pn"t(S)u:.ki]; Pantluci ["pan.tS
<
U.ci]) is an ex-
perimental constructed language designed to explore the bounds
of what a human-usable language can be by using a breadth-rst
search inspired heavily esoteric grammar similar to the grammar
of few if any natural or constructed languages. To make up for the
unfamiliarity, it uses a largely streamline grammar that attempts to
minimize the number of things a speaker or listener needs to think
about.
The language was developed beginning in late June 2021 fol-
lowing various sketches of potential breadth-rst languages inspired
by xkcd 2407. It originally used a modied consonant inventory
from unrelated language sketches from December 2020, a vowel
inventory copied from Lojban, a tone system copied from Ladan,
and a grammar system heavily inspired by toki pona. By early
2022 most of these inuences were largely obscured by revisions
to the language in an attempt to minimize the chances of sound
changes, and the wide variety of grammatical particles were reduced
as much as possible. By mid June 2022 the now sole particle used
in the language was extremely repetitive and that became became
a larger and larger problem forcing a redo of the morphology and
phonology in order to allow for reduplication and a greater focus
on aesthetics. By July 2022, the language expanded into a research
project exploring esoteric languages in general, with the language
used as an example. Its phonology was once again scrapped and
rebuilt with a focus on lling the same purpose as the grammar. By
January 2023 its minimal inventory had made reduplication equally
as repetitive, and it was changed to a rst vowel umlaut system
that mirrored the recently implemented compounding system. By
May 2023, the scientic nature of the exploration was abandoned
in favor of a more artistic approach in order to better appeal to
the Authors strengths and target audience. Over the course of
its development it went through the names Hyban ([@.`@.b

8.2.4 Morphology
Pantlucis syllables may be analyzed as having a form anywhere
from (C)V(m), (an optional consonant followed by a vowel or syl-
labic consonant followed by an optional nasal coda). /p/, /t/, and
/k/ may act as though they were syllables during word construc-
tion. The sequences /aa/, /pp/, /ttC/ (where C is a non-syllabic
consonant) , and /kk/ do not occur. Syllables with both syllabic
consonants and codas do not occur. No words end with /p/, /t/, or
/k/. Ambiguous null onsets (always root initial) are marked with
h. Stress rests on the rst syllable containing a vowel. Words
must contain at least one vowel. Syllabic consonants may not occur
anywhere in a word before the stressed vowel.
Content words in Pantluci undergo a morphological process in
order to denote the grammatical structure of the sentence. This
process consists of replacing the rst vowel sequence with /a/ if
there are 0 preceeding nodes, /i/ if there is 1, then as the number
increases /u/, /ai/, /au/, /aia/, /aua/, etc.

Interjections have any other intial vowel sequence: /ia/, /iu/, /ua/,

8.4 Compounding
8.4.1 Joining
The actual morphological process for compounding has a few stages
depending on how useful and common the semantics of the compound
end up being.
1. loaning (incl self loaning) loans can have letters added or removed
based on commonness apaplca => apaca
2. The most basic form of compounding is to take two or more roots,
then for each of the roots other than the rst one, if the rst
vowel has a nasal coda remove the vowel, otherwise replace the
vowel with an l. This turns them into syllabic consonants due
their position. Then attach all of the roots together in order. If
any of the syllabic ns or ls would cause phonemes from the
other roots to attach to them then place an h between those
two roots in order to specify where the syllable boundary is.
