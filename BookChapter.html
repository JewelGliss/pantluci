8. Pantluci an esoteric human language
8.1 Overview
Pantluci (English [pæn"t(S)ôu:.ki]; Pantluci ["p˘an.tlu.ci]) is an ex-
perimental constructed language designed to explore the bounds
of what a human-usable language can be by using a breadth-ﬁrst
search inspired heavily esoteric grammar which appears in few if
any natural or constructed languages. To make up for the unfa-
miliarity, it uses a largely streamline grammar that attempts to
minimize the number of things a speaker or listener needs to think
about.
The language was developed beginning in late June 2021 fol-
lowing various sketches of potential breadth-ﬁrst languages inspired
by xkcd 2407. It originally used a modiﬁed consonant inventory
from unrelated language sketches from December 2020, a vowel
inventory copied from Lojban, a tone system copied from L´aadan,
and a grammar system heavily inspired by toki pona. By early 2022
most of these inﬂuences were largely obscured by revisions to the
language in an attempt to minimize the chances of sound changes,
and the wide variety of grammatical particles were reduced as much
as possible. By mid June 2022 the now sole particle used in the
language being extremely repetitive became a larger and larger
problem forcing a redo of the phonology in order to allow for less
repetitive forms and a greater focus on aesthetics. By July 2022,
the language expanded into a research project exploring esoteric
languages in general, with the language used as an example. Its
phonology was once again scrapped and rebuilt with a focus on
ﬁlling the same purpose as the grammar. By January 2023 its
minimal inventory had made reduplication equally as repetitive,
and it was changed to a ﬁrst vowel umlaut system that mirrored
the recently implemented compounding system. Over the course
of its development it went through the names H´yyban ([ç´@.`@.b

8.2.3 Morphology
Syllables in Pantluci are of the form (t)(C)V(n), i.e. an optional /t/
followed by an optional consonant followed by a vowel or syllabic conso-
nant followed by an optional nasal coda. Words may be any combination
of syllables with these exceptions: /p/, /t/, or /k/ may act as though
they were syllables during word construction. /pp/, and /kk/ are illegal
sequences. /tt/ may not occur before a non-syllabic consonant. Syllables
with syllabic consonants may not have a coda. Words may not end with
/p/, /t/, or /k/. Ambiguous null onsets must be marked with 〈h〉. Stress
rests on the ﬁrst syllable containing a vowel. Words must contain at
least one vowel. The same vowel may not occur twice in a row. Syllabic
consonants may not occur anywhere in a word before the stressed vowel.
Content words in Pantluci undergo a morphological process in order
to denote the grammatical structure of the sentence. This process consists
of replacing the ﬁrst vowel sequence with /a/ if there are 0 preceeding
nodes, /i/ if there is 1, then as the number increases /u/, /ai/, /au/,
/aia/, /aua/, etc.
pantluci → pintluci → puntluci → paintluci → ...
Interjections have any other intial vowel sequence: /ia/, /iu/, /ua/, /ui/,
etc.
