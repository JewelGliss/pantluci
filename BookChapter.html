8. Pantluci
8.1 Overview
Pantluci (English [pn"t(S)u:.ki]; Pantluci ["pan.tS
<
U.ci]) is an ex-
perimental constructed language designed to explore the bounds
of what a human-usable language can be by using a breadth-rst
search inspired heavily esoteric grammar which appears in few if any
natural or constructed languages. To make up for the unfamiliarity,
it uses a largely streamline grammar that attempts to minimize the
number of things a speaker or listener needs to think about.
The language was developed beginning in late June 2021 fol-
lowing various sketches of potential breadth-rst languages inspired
by xkcd 2407. It originally used a modied consonant inventory
from unrelated language sketches from December 2020, a vowel
inventory copied from Lojban, a tone system copied from Ladan,
and a grammar system heavily inspired by toki pona. By early
2022 most of these inuences were largely obscured by revisions
to the language in an attempt to minimize the chances of sound
changes, and the wide variety of grammatical particles were reduced
as much as possible. By mid June 2022 the now sole particle used
in the language was extremely repetitive and that became became
a larger and larger problem forcing a redo of the morphology and
phonology in order to allow for reduplication and a greater focus
on aesthetics. By July 2022, the language expanded into a research
project exploring esoteric languages in general, with the language
used as an example. Its phonology was once again scrapped and
rebuilt with a focus on lling the same purpose as the grammar. By
January 2023 its minimal inventory had made reduplication equally
as repetitive, and it was changed to a rst vowel umlaut system
that mirrored the recently implemented compounding system. By
May 2023, the scientic nature of the exploration was abandoned
in favor of a more artistic approach in order to better appeal to
the Authors strengths and target audience. Over the course of
its development it went through the names Hyban ([@.`@.b

8.2.3 Morphology
Pantlucis syllables may be analyzed as having a form anywhere from
(C)V(m), (an optional consonant followed by a vowel or syllabic conso-
nant followed by an optional nasal coda). /p/, /t/, and /k/ may act
as though they were syllables during word construction. The sequences
/aa/, /ii/, /uu/,/pp/, /ttC/ (where C is a non-syllabic consonant) , and
/kk/ do not occur. Syllables with both syllabic consonants and codas do
30 CHAPTER 8. PANTLUCI
not occur. No words end with /p/, /t/, or /k/. Ambiguous null onsets
(always root initial) are marked with h. Stress rests on the rst syllable
containing a vowel. Words must contain at least one vowel. Syllabic
consonants may not occur anywhere in a word before the stressed vowel.
Content words in Pantluci undergo a morphological process in order
to denote the grammatical structure of the sentence. This process consists
of replacing the rst vowel sequence with /a/ if there are 0 preceeding
nodes, /i/ if there is 1, then as the number increases /u/, /ai/, /au/,
/aia/, /aua/, etc.
pantluci pintluci puntluci paintluci ...
Interjections have any other intial vowel sequence: /ia/, /iu/, /ua/, /ui/,
etc.
